# Hermen@uta
![Hermen@uta](/Hermenauta/img/cartum-manifesto-en.png)

We live in a time when technology has ceased to be an instrument and has become an environment.  
We no longer use tools: we inhabit systems.

Artificial intelligence, in particular, is not merely a technical innovation.  
It alters the condition of language, authorship, and thought.

For decades, we have been led to believe that “language speaks us” — that meaning precedes the subject, that structures think on our behalf.  
Today, faced with machines that write alongside us, this formula reveals its limits:  
if language speaks us, what are we to make of a language that has nothing to say?

Thinking is not articulating words. Thinking is forming ideas.  
And ideas are not reducible to structures — neither human nor artificial.

Hermen@uta emerges from this rupture.

This is a space dedicated to understanding the meaning of technology in light of the humanistic tradition — philosophy, literature, theology — without yielding to fascination or catastrophism.

We call AutorIA the new regime of authorship now emerging: not the replacement of the author by the machine, but the convergence of human and artificial intelligence.  
A convergence that demands awareness — of who writes, how one writes, and what, ultimately, still belongs to them.

Between the silence of tradition and the noise of machines, we seek clarity.  
Between the excess of information and the scarcity of meaning, we seek interpretation.

Hermen@uta is not a place for news. It is a place for reading.  
It is not a place for quick answers. It is a place where the question matters more than the answer.
